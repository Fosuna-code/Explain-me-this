Calculus is a branch of mathematics that deals with the study of change. It is used to find the rate of change of a function (called the derivative) and the area under a curve (called the integral). Calculus is used to solve problems in many areas of science, engineering, economics, and medicine. It is also used to model physical phenomena, such as the motion of objects. Calculus can be thought of as a toolbox of techniques that can be used to solve problems involving change.
